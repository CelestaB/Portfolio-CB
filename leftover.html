Leftover Rescue

Case Study - the concept and creation of an app to reduce food waste

The Problem

We are working to reduce the amount of food waste, created by restaurants specifically, in addition to providing affordable meals to the local community.

The Solution

We created an app that notifies health and budget-conscious people when a restaurant is about to throw away edible, excess food.

Previous Next

We took a pretty traditional approach and kicked off the project with our user research. We worked through user research which lead us to the definition phase, and finished with creating the prototype. This project was conducted as a part of the UX/UI certificate course through the University of Minnesota. While this project was not implemented by NEMAA at this time, we did discuss with their team about how the project could progress in the future.

User Research

Our user research commenced the project. I planned and conducted the user interviews, created the user scenario and storyboarded the user path. We interviewed artist, community members and a stakeholder to discover what route to take to encompass all aspects of the non-profit's growth.

Definition

During our definition phase, I took the lead on the competitor analysis and heuristics evaluation which aided me in discovering our key insights, problem statement and value proposition. These findings were key to measuring our progress and successfulness in our process.

Prototyping

For our prototyping phase, I created the style tile and focused on A/B testing plans, the clickable aspects of XD, and the homepage. My main contributions in our final stages were iterating the changes from our mid to high-fidelity wireframes.
