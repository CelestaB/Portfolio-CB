Leftover Rescue

Case Study - the concept and creation of an app to reduce food waste

The Problem

We are working to reduce the amount of food waste, created by restaurants specifically, in addition to providing affordable meals to the local community.

The Solution

We created an app that notifies health and budget-conscious people when a restaurant is about to throw away edible, excess food.

Previous Next

For this project, we were assigned the task of coming up with an idea for an app and then executing the initial research and design needed to back our concept. We wanted the app to be useful in our community and affect change in someway. After throwing around some ideas, we landed on the concept of Leftover Rescue. It would be an app that local restaurants could use to list food at the end of the night that would otherwise be wasted at a discounted price. Our aim was to focus on college students and lower income individuals who would benefit from a low-budget meal that was not neceassarily fast-food, and reduce food waste at the same time. Once we hashed out the details of what exactly we wanted the app to do, we began user research to see exactly who would use/benefit most from the app. From there, we continued to narrow our ideas on to what would truly be pheasible and ended up with a few beginning versions of our prototype.

User Research

As stated above, once the concept was decided we began with our user research. We had many grandious ideas for our initial concept which meant it would target a variety of different demographics.Through our findings, however, we needed to narrow down the user to a specific individual. We were continually reminded throughout our process that you must start with precise ideas and then as the company grows; those ideas can be implimented.

Definition

After we had a focused idea of who we were creating the app for, it was much easier to work through the definition phase. I took the lead on the competitive analysis. The most blantant discovery I found was that there were no competitors in our area. While there were apps that had the same idea most were in Europe or large metropolitan areas. Due to that fact, we were able to gain a lot of ideas from what works well for them and indiviualize them to fit our needs.

Prototyping

The protoryping phase is always my favorite. It's gratifying to finally see a product come to life. We tested a few differnt prototypes starting with very lo-fi and working our way through some A/B testing to create higher-fidelity options. Through testing we found that our colors were not color compliant, as well as, where each button was most effective.
